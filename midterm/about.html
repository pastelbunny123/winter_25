What is Winter?

Winter is the coldest season of the year. Around this time, there is less daylight and longer nights. During winter, there are many different holidays and activities that people all around the world participate in. Some holidays include Christmas, Hannukah, Lunar New Year, and Kwanzaa. Some popular activities include skiing, ice skating, sledding, and snowshoeing. Winter is at different times of the year in the Northern and Southern Hemispheres, but these celebrations are all celebrated from December 21st to March 20th, during the Northern Hemisphere's winter season. Winter Holidays:

A History

Several cultures throughout history have celebrated Winter, and the winter solstice. Special foods, festivals, and monuments have been created to honor the season by many religions and groups of people for thousands of years, making this season an important and substantial time of celebration for many. The Winter Olympics

One example of a large and worldwide celebration of Winter is the Winter Olympic Games. The first Winter Olympics were held in 1924 in the French Alps and included several of the popular Winter sports included in the photos page. This first version of the Games only included six original sports, four of which were forms of skiing or skating. This small number has grown to 15 sports as of the last Winter Olympic Games.
